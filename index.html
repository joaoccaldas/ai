VI · Connectors10 / 16
Prompt pattern

Weak prompt: “Analyze this.” Strong prompt: role, context, output and uncertainty.

The output is not the final answer. It is a high-quality first draft for finance review.

Act as an FP&A business partner. Analyze this dataset with Actuals, Budget, FC1, FC2 and LY. Return: 1. Top five variance drivers 2. Any anomalies or data-quality issues 3. First-pass executive commentary 4. Recommended chart 5. Questions I should ask the business owner Separate facts from interpretation. Flag uncertainty.
VII · Prompts11 / 16
Hacker prompt
Using the close checklist, prior month evidence folder and current status file: 1. Identify missing evidence 2. Flag high-risk reconciliations 3. Summarize blockers by owner 4. Draft follow-up messages 5. Create an exception list for finance review Do not mark anything complete without source evidence.

A chatbot answers. A workflow checks, connects, drafts, flags and waits for approval.

This is where AI becomes a control surface, not just a text box.

VII · Prompts12 / 16
Governance

Treat AI like a brilliant junior intern.

Give context, demand sources, review output and publish as human. Finance owns the conclusion.

Governed AI checklistApproved tools · source citations · permission review · data classification · audit trail · human approval · clear workspace policy.
VIII · Governance13 / 16
God Mode

Do not only ask: can AI help with this task? Ask: should this task exist in this form?

At maturity, AI-native finance becomes a redesign of rhythm, ownership, controls and decision flow.

Dynamic revenue waterfallBookings, pipeline, risk signals, discounts, regions, products and channels feed a living forecast narrative rather than a static monthly snapshot.
IX · Operating model14 / 16
The one-level rule

Do not ask teams to become AI-native in one heroic leap.

Move one level up. Start with repeated pain, create reusable prompts, validate them and turn what works into organizational memory.

One taskPick a repeated manual activity.
One promptMake the first reusable recipe.
One weekShare what worked and what failed.
